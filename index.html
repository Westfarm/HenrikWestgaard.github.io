About me

I'm 31 years old from Drammen, and I recently finished my degree for electrical engineering at Oslomet. I moved to Oslo in 2018 after spending a year abroad in Australia. In Australia I lived throughout the south-east from Melbourne to Sydney, mainly staying in Canberra for work. Here I strengthened an already native level fluency and learned to apply myself in a new environment entirely on my own....

I spent a lot of time trying to decide on what I wanted to do in life. This led me to pursue a broad field of interest. From graphical design, literature, philosophy and psychology to electronics, computers and engineering.

I have from an early age been curious of how things work, from the physical to the metaphysical, and this curiosity eventually carried over into my studies.
Having struggled to find motivation and interest in school during my final years at VGS, i dropped out to commit to skiing and gymnastics, but suffering an injury that required surgery and rehabilitation made me rethink. Knowing I eventually would want to go to university meant I had to finish my VGS education, so that's what I did. Over the next few years I took all the required subjects for social science "vitnemål" while working part time.

Most of my adult life I have in some capacity been helping my father run his physiotherapi clinic. He suffers from Ushers Syndrome, which is a neurodegenerative condition that affects his sight and hearing. His condition means he's slowly losing both sight and hearing rendering him blind and partially deaf over time. Despite his condition he is the sole proprietor of Åssiden Fysioterapi. To help ease the difficulties of his condition I have taken on the position of assistant. This means I do most of the paperwork and finances, as well as general manegement, patient journals and booking.

In 2015 I decided I wanted to do something related to science, and started working on the required subjects of maths and physics to apply for higher education in that field. I finished all my exams for the required science subjects by 2016, where I got the opertunity to move to Australia for a year to experience living abroad.
This year let me travel and meet new people. Despite not having any studies or job lined up before departure I managed to find work and a network of friends within a short amount of time. As I was on a working holiday visa I was limited to a maximum of 6 months employment from any single employer. This gave me the opertunity to try different types of work in entirely different areas, helping me build a broader experience and understanding of culture.

Upon my return to Norway I had decided I wanted to persue an electrical engineering degree. So I worked in Drammen, at PostNord until I had saved up enough to move to Oslo. In Oslo I first took a precourse to engineering, working through the basics of university maths and science, as well as more elaborate understanding of how engineers work and operate. Upon completing the precourse I applied for and was accepted to electrical engineering at Oslomet.

At Oslomet I made an effort to partake in all optional offers for social and academic endevours, creating a large network of friends and getting on good terms with professors and faculty right from the start. Within the first year of study I had already made lasting relationships and went traveling abroad with fellow studnets and friends. Having been used to spending large amounts of time at campus sosialising and working with other people the Covid pandemic made this impossible. I therefore joined the creation of the student organisation "The Resistance". Here I took on the role as event organiser and was a member of the board. The idea of this organisation was to unite the students for engineering at Oslomet and give them a forum for communication and support despite the quarantine that was implemented. We hosted social events, connected students across subjects and year to help each other and to build networks. We contacted businesses like Siemens, Vingmed and ABB and hosted presentations where people from the industry could present their companies and get in contact with students.

Electrical engineers at Oslomet have to choose between medical or industrial automation. Wanting to work with automation, controllers, systems and programming, I chose industrial automation where I took robotics, artificial intelligence, and mathematics 4 as my optional subjects for my final year along with my bachelor thesis.

For my thesis I worked with three other students, and we contacted ABB where we were selected amongst a large number of applicants to work on their project as our thesis. The project was the continuation of a previous thesis that had been done by students at Oslomet the previous year. We were tasked with creating an autonomous system capable of unmanned inspection of remote installations, and given free reign on how we wanted to change or implement on the system. The previous project had laid the foundation with a camera mounted system that moved linearly along a rail, capable of doing routine inspections at timed intervals. Initially wanting to do a camera drone system, we resigned to continue prototyping on the rail system, as government regulations on professional drone systems using artificial intelligence and image recognition needed to be supervised and permits + background checks on everyone involved in the project would need to be done. These permits could take up to 6 months to get, meaning our deadline for thesis would be over before we could even begin the project.
If you want to read more about the thesis I'll provide a link further down on the website.

In my free time I enjoy working on DIY projects, anything invovling home automation, fixing electronics or designing. I do my own 3D printing and 3D modeling, PCB design, circuits and soldering. The design aspect carries over into an interest in art and painting, where I sketch, draw and paint both analog and digital, both recreation and for work. I have broad experience in drawing up schematics and operate seamlessly between digital and analog which means I'm good at realising projects. I am also a big fan of literature, cinema, photography and gaming. To stay active I play golf, disc golf, tennis, mountain bike in the summer and ski in the winter. I enjoy traveling and frequently visit Spain where I have extended family. This has prompted me to learn how to speak spanish fluently, and I'm currently at a B1 level.
